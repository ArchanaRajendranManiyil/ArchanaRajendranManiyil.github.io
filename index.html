Personal Story

From Excel Jockey to Data Analyst: My Turning Point in Analytics

🔍 The Struggle: A few years ago, I was drowning in spreadsheets—copy-pasting, reconciling reports manually, and chasing down data inconsistencies every month-end. It was repetitive, slow, and frustrating.

🔄 The Turning Point: One day, I spent 3 hours fixing a report that broke due to a tiny formula error. That night, I decided it was time to automate or drown. I taught myself Power Query and R—not just to make things work, but to understand why they broke.

⚡ The Transformation:

  • I automated a recurring financial report using R markdown and Power BI, reducing hours of work to just 10 minutes.
  • I started using sentiment analysis on regulatory news to flag potential risk items ahead of time.
  • Stakeholders stopped asking "Where’s the report?" and started asking "How did you find that insight?"

📈 The Impact: That shift didn’t just save time. It built trust. It showed initiative. And it eventually landed me a dedicated role in analytics.

💬 If you’re stuck in repetitive tasks—know this: You don’t need a degree to transition into analytics. You need curiosity, consistency, and the courage to break things (and fix them better).
